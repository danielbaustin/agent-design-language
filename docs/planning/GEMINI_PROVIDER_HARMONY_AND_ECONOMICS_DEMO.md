# Gemini Provider Harmony and Economics Demo

## Status

Delivered baseline / planning provenance.

The bounded v0.89 and v0.89.1 Gemini/provider-harmony demos have landed. This
document is kept as source context for any future cost-aware provider-routing or
provider-participation follow-on, not as an active next-milestone commitment.

## Purpose

Preserve the next meaningful Gemini-centered demo direction beyond the bounded
`Gemini in the Loop` slice.

This document describes a future demo where Gemini is not merely a bounded
packet reviewer, but a visible participant in ADL's broader provider-harmony
story and, eventually, in the economics/accountability story that sits behind
provider choice.

## Why This Exists

`Gemini in the Loop` already proves something important:

- ADL can host Gemini truthfully as a bounded participant
- ADL can own packet construction, validation, and artifact writing
- Gemini can contribute without pretending to have unrestricted repo autonomy

That is a strong MVP.

What it does not yet prove is the larger strategic story:

- why Gemini matters to ADL as a community participant
- how provider diversity relates to cost, routing, and choice
- how ADL can host different providers without benchmark-theater or vendor bias

This future demo should preserve that larger direction.

## Core Thesis

The future Gemini demo should show:

`ADL helps different providers participate well, and provider choice is shaped by capability, trust, and economics rather than by vendor lock-in.`

This is not a "Gemini versus everyone else" demo.
It is a "Gemini belongs here too" demo.

## Lineage And Acknowledgement

This future demo should explicitly acknowledge the work that already shaped the
Gemini path:

- the bounded `Gemini in the Loop` `v0.89` demo
- the earlier provider-harmony planning notes that framed Gemini, Claude, and
  ChatGPT as welcomed participants rather than rivals
- the broader repo conversations about harmony, fairness, and treating
  providers as first-class participants with different strengths

The point is continuity, not reinvention.

This matters because the right public story is:

- previous people already did the careful bounded Gemini groundwork
- this demo deepens that direction instead of pretending to discover it

## Demo Shape

The next Gemini-focused demo should probably be a bounded comparative or
roundtable surface with one explicit economics/routing dimension.

Recommended shape:

1. ADL prepares one bounded packet.
2. ADL shows why Gemini is a reasonable participant for that packet.
3. ADL records the routing rationale using:
   - capability
   - cost or cost-class
   - reviewability
   - provider fit
4. Gemini contributes its bounded analysis or synthesis.
5. ADL records the resulting artifact and the provider-selection reason.

## What "Economics" Means Here

This demo should use "economics" carefully.

For now it should mean:

- provider choice has tradeoffs
- ADL can explain why one provider was selected
- those tradeoffs may include cost, latency, capability, safety, and artifact
  quality

It should not claim that the repo already has a finished market or settlement
system.

So the right framing is:

- economics as accountable provider choice
- not economics as a fully realized payment platform

## Recommended Future Variants

### Variant A: Gemini Cost-Aware Packet Routing

ADL chooses Gemini for one bounded task and records why Gemini is the best fit
for that packet under a simple capability/cost policy.

This is the safest next slice.

### Variant B: Gemini in a Provider Roundtable

Gemini participates alongside Claude and ChatGPT in one calm bounded discussion
or review packet, with ADL recording not just the outputs but the selection
rationale and provider-fit notes.

### Variant C: Gemini as a Budget-Friendly Specialist

ADL positions Gemini as the right participant for one class of bounded tasks:

- doc critique
- milestone consistency review
- structured synthesis

The runtime then makes that assignment explicit and reviewer-friendly.

## Recommended Artifacts

Any follow-on Gemini community/economics demo should emit:

- `provider_selection_manifest.json`
- `packet_manifest.json`
- `capability_and_cost_reasoning.md`
- `gemini_artifact.md` or equivalent structured output
- `runtime_summary.json`

That way the demo proves:

- Gemini did something bounded and useful
- ADL selected Gemini for a reason
- the reason is legible afterward

## Recommended Future Slice

If this line is reopened, the next slice should be:

`Build a bounded Gemini provider-fit demo that records capability-and-cost-aware routing rationale and a calm reviewer-facing artifact.`

That keeps the scope honest and strong.

## Non-goals

This future demo should not become:

- a benchmark war
- an anti-Google or pro-Google positioning stunt
- a claim that Gemini already has unrestricted ADL runtime powers
- an economics system that outruns the actual repo substrate

## Success Criteria

This future Gemini slice succeeds if:

- it clearly builds on `Gemini in the Loop`
- it acknowledges the prior provider-harmony framing
- it makes provider choice more legible
- it still treats Gemini as a welcomed participant rather than a compatibility
  guest

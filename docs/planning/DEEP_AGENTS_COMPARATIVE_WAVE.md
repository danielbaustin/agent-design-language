# Deep Agents Comparative Wave

## Purpose

Preserve and deepen the larger comparative/public-positioning story around
deep-agent demos and ADL beyond the bounded `v0.88` comparative proof row.

This is a future-demo planning surface, not a benchmark report.

## Why This Exists

The `v0.88` comparative row already proved something useful:

- ADL can take a visible multi-agent packet
- preserve provenance and reference mapping
- present a bounded reviewer-facing audit surface

That slice was intentionally small and supporting.

What it did not try to do was carry the broader story about:

- why deep-agent-style demos attract attention
- what ADL adds beyond visible file outputs
- how to position ADL publicly without falling into shallow scorecards

This document preserves that broader next step.

## Lineage And Acknowledgement

This future comparative wave should explicitly acknowledge the earlier work it
depends on:

- the bounded `v0.88` `Deep Agents Comparative Proof`
- the `v0.88` demo matrix and reviewer package that gave that proof row a real
  place in the milestone story
- the broader design conversations that pushed the repo toward reviewer-facing
  proof instead of vague “agent magic”

This matters because the future comparative story should feel like a deepening
of prior careful work, not a fresh round of hype.

## Core Comparative Thesis

The future comparative wave should make one public-facing claim legible:

`ADL turns visible multi-agent behavior into a more reviewable, more governable, and more evidence-rich system than a filesystem-only demo surface.`

That is the right level of ambition.

It avoids:

- fake scorecards
- broad claims about winning every benchmark
- cheap shots at other projects

## What The Next Comparative Wave Should Add

The bounded `v0.88` row focused on:

- provenance
- reference mapping
- reviewer auditability

The next wave should deepen the story by showing one or more of:

- explicit runtime policy and stop conditions
- clearer human/operator role visibility
- richer artifact lineage across a multi-step flow
- stronger explanation of what the reviewer can verify directly
- a more legible contrast between “files exist” and “system behavior is
  inspectable”

## Recommended Future Demo Shapes

### Variant A: Comparative Review Packet

ADL prepares the same visible packet in two conceptual forms:

- filesystem-style visible output
- ADL reviewer-facing runtime proof package

The demo then makes the difference explicit without pretending the comparison
is universal.

### Variant B: Comparative Workflow Governance Packet

ADL shows the same multi-agent shape, but with:

- bounded policy
- stop conditions
- visible decision surfaces

This would deepen the public-positioning story meaningfully.

### Variant C: Comparative Human-In-The-Loop Packet

ADL makes the operator’s role more visible than a typical deep-agent filesystem
demo and shows how that changes accountability and reviewability.

## Recommended Artifact Package

A future comparative wave should probably emit:

- `comparative_wave_manifest.json`
- `comparison_claims.md`
- `reviewer_checklist.md`
- `adl_surface_map.json`
- `filesystem_surface_map.json`
- `public_positioning_summary.md`

That gives both reviewer and public-facing surfaces without needing raw debate
copy.

## Tone Rules

The comparative wave should stay:

- calm
- specific
- artifact-grounded
- respectful of previous work by others

It should not become:

- a benchmark ladder
- a “who is smarter” essay
- a winner/loser narrative

## Recommended Next-Milestone Slice

The first good promotion would be:

`Build one reviewer-facing comparative wave package that deepens the v0.88 proof row with stronger public-positioning language and clearer governance/review surfaces.`

That is enough to move the story forward without turning it into a marketing detour.

## Success Criteria

This future comparative wave succeeds if:

- it clearly cites the bounded `v0.88` row as its predecessor
- it widens the comparative story without losing discipline
- a reviewer can explain the ADL difference in a few minutes
- it remains grounded in visible artifacts rather than rhetoric alone

# Vision - v0.90.4

v0.90.4 gives the CSM polis its first bounded economic agency surface.

The aim is not to build a payment network. The aim is to prove that governed
agents can request work, offer work, evaluate bids, award responsibility,
delegate bounded tasks, integrate outputs, and leave behind evidence that a
reviewer can understand.

The milestone should feel like the first working marketplace inside the polis:
small, careful, inspectable, and authority-aware.

## Success Image

A reviewer can open one contract-market proof packet and see:

- a contract with clear parties, scope, constraints, timeline, trace
  requirements, and evaluation criteria
- multiple bids with declared costs, commitments, exceptions, confidence, and
  signed trace requirements
- a selection artifact explaining why a bid was awarded
- authority transitions for open, bidding, awarded, accepted, executing,
  delegated, completed, disputed, failed, or cancelled states
- a subcontract that is linked to the parent contract without hiding parent
  responsibility
- external counterparty evidence that is explicit about trust and limits
- any tool-mediated requirement recorded as a contract constraint, not as
  permission to execute a tool
- a final review summary that distinguishes proof, judgment, warnings, and
  residual risk

## What This Enables

v0.90.4 prepares ADL for later work on resource stewardship, reputation,
inter-polis coordination, payments, and real service markets. It also gives
CodeBuddy, Aptitude Atlas, and future ADL-powered businesses a clean primitive:
a governed contract for bounded work with traceable outputs.

## What It Must Avoid

This milestone must not pretend that a contract-market proof is a complete
economy. It must not blur citizen standing, governance authority, or private
state boundaries. It must not import real payment rails before the authority and
evidence model is trustworthy.

It must also avoid treating tool calls as an incidental implementation detail.
Contracts may need tools, but governed tool-call semantics belong to v0.90.5.

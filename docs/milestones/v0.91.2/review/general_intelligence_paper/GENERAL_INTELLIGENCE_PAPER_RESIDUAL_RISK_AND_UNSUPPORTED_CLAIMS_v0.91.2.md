# General Intelligence Paper Residual Risk And Unsupported Claims

## Residual Risks

- Full theorem-by-theorem mathematical audit is still outstanding.
- Several high-level implications remain persuasive framing rather than settled
  demonstrated results.
- The manuscript still benefits from toy models or worked examples that show
  the metric behaves sensibly.
- Computability and approximation treatment for `CCC` still needs stronger
  explicit discussion.
- Task-encoding and task-distribution dependence still need careful reviewer
  framing to avoid accidental overclaim.

## Unsupported Or Weakened Claim Areas

- "All problem solving is state-space compression."
- "The metric is unbiased."
- "The framework eliminates benchmark dependence."
- "The framework already demonstrates cross-species or cross-substrate
  comparability."
- "We have shown a complete mathematical theory of general intelligence."

## Safer Replacements

- "Many problem-solving processes can be modeled as task-relevant state-space
  compression."
- "The framework is less anthropomorphic when task, cost, and validation
  assumptions are explicit."
- "The framework makes benchmark dependence explicit through task distributions
  and validation surfaces."
- "The framework offers a candidate comparison method under declared task
  families and resource policies."
- "The paper proposes a mathematical framework for intelligence as
  compression-efficient problem solving."

## Mechanical Risks Now Reduced

- bibliography-gap failure is no longer the dominant blocker
- stale old-repo path references inside the active paper packet docs have been
  reduced
- the manuscript source is no longer split ambiguously between two active homes

# Vision - v0.90.3

v0.90.3 protects the continuity-bearing core of a citizen.

The milestone should shift ADL from provisional citizen JSON records toward a
durable citizen-state substrate: typed private state, signed state envelopes,
append-only lineage, redacted projections, explicit standing, and conservative
behavior when continuity becomes ambiguous.

## Why It Matters

Once a system can admit citizens, wake them, and show them to operators, their
state becomes morally and technically sensitive. A citizen should not be
silently forked, rewritten, inspected, resumed from ambiguous lineage, or shown
through an unredacted operator god view.

v0.90.3 does not prove personhood or birth. It makes later personhood and birth
claims safer by refusing to treat editable prototype artifacts as durable
identity.

## What Success Looks Like

By the end of v0.90.3, reviewers should be able to inspect one bounded
citizen-state proof and answer:

- what is authoritative private state
- which projections are safe views
- which key or trust-root policy authorized a write
- what lineage led to the current checkpoint
- why a wake is accepted, degraded, rejected, or quarantined
- whether an equivocation attempt was detected
- what the citizen-facing continuity receipt says
- what the Observatory may see without reading raw private state
- who has standing to communicate, inspect, challenge, or affect state

## Non-Claims

- v0.90.3 does not birth the first true Gödel agent.
- v0.90.3 does not complete the moral or emotional civilization layer.
- v0.90.3 does not complete migration, birthday, or identity rebinding.
- v0.90.3 does not implement the full economics or contract-market substrate.
- v0.90.3 does not make cloud enclaves mandatory.

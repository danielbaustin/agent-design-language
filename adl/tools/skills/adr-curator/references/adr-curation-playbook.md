# ADR Curation Playbook

Use this reference when tightening ADR candidates after the deterministic
scaffold has produced a first pass.

## Candidate Quality Checklist

- The title describes one decision, not a broad theme.
- The status is explicit and conservative.
- Proposed decisions are visibly proposed.
- Accepted decisions cite source evidence that says they were accepted.
- Context explains the pressure or tradeoff that created the decision.
- Decision text states the chosen direction without adding unsupported claims.
- Consequences include at least one benefit and one cost, risk, or follow-up.
- Alternatives are either source-grounded or marked as inferred candidate
  alternatives.
- Supersession links name the replaced or replacing ADR when evidence supports
  the relationship.
- Validation notes say what evidence was checked and what remains unproven.

## Boundary Checklist

- Do not accept proposed ADRs.
- Do not edit existing ADR files.
- Do not create repository docs.
- Do not create issues or PRs.
- Do not turn ADR drafting into architecture remediation.
- Do not infer historical intent beyond the source evidence.
